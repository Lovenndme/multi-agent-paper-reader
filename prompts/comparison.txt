Comparability rules:
- Use "direct" only when task, dataset, metric, split, and evaluation conditions are sufficiently aligned.
- Use "conditional" when a useful comparison is possible but important conditions differ.
- Use "not_comparable" when values or claims should not be ranked directly.
- For conditional or non-comparable dimensions, explain the exact reason in warning.
- Do not infer that a larger number is better unless the metric direction and conditions are clear.

- critique: prioritize originality, strengths, limitations, broader impact, applicability, and research gaps.
- custom: build dimensions that directly answer the user's stated focus while retaining comparability warnings.
